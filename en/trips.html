The most beautiful beaches of the north coast

Las Teresitas
The pearl of Tenerife, the emblematic beach of the island and one of the most beautiful beaches in Spain, has a 1.5 km long arc of fine golden sand with beautiful palm trees and an artificial reef that ensures a calm sea regardless of the wind direction. Of course there are sun loungers, showers, restaurants and sports facilities. The beach has ideal conditions for snorkelling, the artificial reef is home to many colourful fish
El Bollullo
Just 30 minutes walk from Puerto de la Cruz you will find El Bollullo, one of the most beautiful beaches in Tenerife. This natural beach is surrounded by dramatic cliffs, covered in fine lava sand and washed by intense turquoise water, sometimes with larger waves.
Playa Jardín
The almost 700 m long town beach in Puerto de La Cruz is one of Tenerife's Blue Flag beaches. It is a busy beach with fine black sand, surrounded by a beautiful promenade with exotic vegetation and many restaurants and bars.
Little lakes „Charcos“
Natural lakes in volcanic rocks with clear and warm water provide an extraordinary swimming and snorkelling experience. Most of them can be found on the north coast or in the northeast at Los Gigantes. The most famous are Charco de Isla Cangrejo, Charco del Tancon, Charcodel Viento and Charco de la Laja

The most beautiful beaches of the north coast

La Tejita Beach
The natural beach with light sand (!) is located on the south coast of Tenerife near El Médano. It has amazing natural surroundings, especially the red rocks of Montaña Roja. Due to its location in the open sea there is often wind and bigger waves. A favourite spot for nudists.
Las Vistas 
The most popular beach in Los Cristianos, awarded with the Blue Flag. The beach with fine light sand, almost a kilometre long, is popular with families with children and for its calm shallow water. The beach has water sports facilities and a wide selection of restaurants, cafés and bars.
Los Gigantes
A small but stunning beach on the south-west coast of Tenerife, also known as Los Guios, is surrounded by impressive cliffs reaching heights of up to 600 metres. The fine volcanic sand and shallow, calm water attract romantic couples and families alike to this beach. This beach is a 5-minute walk from the apartment.
Los Cristianos
A beautiful urban, semi-artificial beach with fine sand and a shallow entrance to the water is the main beach of Los Cristianos. There are showers, toilets and a children's playground, and you can rent an umbrella with a sun lounger, pedal boats or windsurfing equipment. The promenade is lined with restaurants, cafés and bars.
Las Americas
Playa de las Americas is the most famous beach in Tenerife. The man-made, large, clean and well-equipped beach with sun loungers and umbrellas is a popular spot for sports enthusiasts from surfing to volleyball and basketball.

Trips

Masca handcuff
The village is situated at an altitude of 650 m in the Macizo de Teno Mountains. The main tourist attraction, apart from the magnificent views, is the trek through the Masca Gorge. A beautiful journey of about 4 hours awaits you on the descent of the Masca Gorge, which is the second most popular activity you can do in Tenerife after the climb up Pico del Teide. The hiking route through the gorge starts in the picturesque mountain village of Masca and ends at the beach of the same name, a short distance from the huge Los Gigantes rock massifs, which are among the largest in Spain. The hike takes you through a beautiful landscape of rocks, steep slopes and flowering prickly pear trees, but it is quite challenging. It climbs over 600 metres in roughly 4 km and passes through a rugged rocky landscape. Although the trail is marked and well trodden, good sturdy shoes and plenty of water and food are a must. You have to return the same way uphill or use the boats on the beach. However, you will be rewarded with views of 100-metre cliffs dropping into the Atlantic Ocean. As of 2021, entry to the Masca Gorge is now regulated and reservations must be made on the official website. There is a fee to enter, but it often sells out more than a month in advance. Public transport buses also run to Masca, 7-9 times a day, including weekends. Both lines 355 and 365 connect Masca with the towns of Santiago del Teide and Buenavista del Norte, where you can transfer to other services around the island.
EL DRAGO MILENARIO
Few visitors to Tenerife miss the sight of the island's oldest and largest tree, called El Drago Milenario, or the Millennium Dragon. Estimated to be between 800 and 1 000 years old, this impressive dragon tree can be found in the north-west of the island and in the town of Icod de los Vinos, which is located in a famous wine-growing region that produces excellent wines and delicious goat's cheese, which you can buy from one of the local farms. This specimen is even the world's oldest representative of the Dragon. Reaching a height of around 21 metres, it has over 300 main branches. In spring it blooms with white and pink flowers. El Drago Milenario is one of the symbols of the Canary Islands and was even depicted on the 1 000 peseta note of the former Spanish currency. The tree stands in the botanical garden of the Parque del Drago, where you can also admire hundreds of other species of plants. The park is open daily from 8:30am to 6:00pm and costs €5 to enter, but you can also see it for free from Parroquia San Marcos, about 100 metres away.
Pico del Teide
One of the most popular tourist destinations that you should definitely not miss when visiting Tenerife is the hike up the highest mountain in Spain, Pico del Teide (3 718 m), which is also an active volcano and a natural symbol of the whole island. Believe me, if you go here, you will not regret it. The unique lunar landscape full of lava formations and sulphur springs is worth seeing, as are the unprecedented views of the ocean and snow-white clouds. The impressive El Teide can be found roughly in the centre of the island in the national park of the same name, which covers 36% of the island. On the way to the top, keep in mind that altitude problems (headaches, difficulty breathing, high intensity of sunlight) may occur.
‍Permission to enter
Permission of the National Park. The permit is issued free of charge, due to the huge demand it must be booked well in advance. Usually permits are sold out 2-3 months in advance! Permits are only needed for the last 700 metres of the route called "Telesforo Bravo" (see route map) from the top station of the cable car to the summit.
There is only one basic hiking route to the top of Pico del Teide .
Montaña Blanca - La Rambleta                  
The starting point is at the car park at a place called Montaña Blanca. The car park is very small and there is often parking in the open spaces along the road. The climb is quite challenging, but the average trained hiker can do it without special equipment. However, good quality and sturdy hiking boots are a must. The first half of the route (about 4 km) is comfortable and only slightly uphill on a gravel road. The second half is steeper and very rocky, where a rather narrow path is trodden. However, you don't have to worry about the downright dangerous sections. You can spend one night in the Refugio de Altavista hut. However, you need to make a reservation in advance. The hut is located at an altitude of 3,271 metres above sea level. After about 8.5 km, you will reach the top station of the cable car, from where you have only a 700m stretch to the top. At the beginning of this section, the national park guards always check the permits.
‍
Distance: 9.5 km one way
Time: approx. 5 hours there + 2.5 hours back
‍Elevation: approx. 1350 metres
‍Difficulty: difficult (no special equipment required)
La Catedral
The second route leads from the rock formations of La Catedral on the other side of the mountain around the top of Pico Viejo. It is longer, gentler, but leads through more difficult terrain, where the path often gets lost among the rocks and sometimes is not at all legible or marked. For this reason too, this path is considerably less frequented.
‍
Distance: 10 km one way
‍Time: approx. 5.5 hours there + 3 hours back
‍Elevation: approx. 1570 metres
‍Difficulty: difficult(no special equipment required)
Lift to Pico del Teide
You can reach the altitude of 3,555 metres by a suspended cable car, which can be reached by car or by bus number 342. The bottom station of the cable car is at 2,356 metres and the entire route to the summit is covered by a pair of modern cabins in less than 8 minutes.
The cable car operates daily throughout the year:
First ride: 9.00 a.m.
Last trip up: 16:00
Last trip down: 16:50

ANAGA RURAL PARK
Anaga is a narrow road with lots of sharp turns and steep passages. It takes quite a bit of time to get to the start of one of the many trails that wind through the park. I therefore recommend that you set aside at least three days to visit the park. Especially if you like hiking, coastal trails, mysterious forests, deep canyons, steep cliffs and secluded beaches.
Roque de Taborno
If you had to choose just one trip in all of Anaga, I recommend this one. The short trek from the village of Taborno includes everything - a wild roadtrip through the heart of Anaga Park, a drive above the canyon, views of the pointed mountains typical of this peninsula, a coastline with cliffs and beaches, and of course a view of the Tenerife Matterhorn. Park in the small village of Taborno. From there, you descend through the forest for a while and continue through a gate along a sheep trail (there was certainly no shortage of poop). To the left, you'll get a view of the deep Barranco de Taborno canyon, and all the while the symbol of this trip, the Roque de Taborno, looms before you, quite reminiscent of the Swiss Matterhorn on the whole. The Roque de Taborno can be circumnavigated in its entirety, it is a very short circuit with minimal elevation gain. We went clockwise and right at the beginning we got stuck on a beautiful viewpoint where you can see down to the coast. There is a black sand beach here called Playa Fajana. Unfortunately, it can only be reached from the other side of the canyon from the village of Las Caboneras. The second time, stay at the Era de los Cardos lookout at the cliff behind Roque de Taborno. From here, the coastline is as far as the eye can see, the iconic hill overhead and the ocean at your back. As they often say, the journey is the destination, and in this case it's doubly true. Walking back will make you wish this trip was longer...
El Draguillo
The hike through El Draguillo is one of the most famous in Anaga Park. But this place has earned its fame and you can enjoy magnificent coastal views. But first you need to get to the trailhead in the village of Benijo, which is a pretty ride that involves about a thousand switchbacks, a tunnel ride and a steep descent down the mountain to sea level. Parking in Benijo isn't exactly the easiest either, as it's pretty limited, plus it's home to the most popular beach in all of Anaga, Playa Benijo. That's why it's better to arrive before 10:00. I recommend starting clockwise, or on the wide path above the cliffs. There is a no-entry policy for tourists, but occasionally a local may pass by. Anyway, this road runs almost flat and in two kilometres you will be in the village of El Draguillo. This is where the fun begins, because in the next one kilometer you will climb all the elevation on the route (maps.cz show 500 meters, which I would believe in this case). Don't forget to go for a swim at Benijo beach.
Faro de Anaga
The village of Chamorga, which is the starting point of this trek, is located at the very end of the road leading through Anaga Park. This adds a touch of wildness and beauty to the whole trip. Although this area is not very busy, you may have a problem with parking after 10:00, which is again very limited. What follows is a fairly intense climb up the hillside above the village of Chamorga. A small trail leads to a viewpoint overlooking the valley and the Roque del Pilón rock formation. From the Pilón it is pretty much flat to the ruins of Casas Tafada. Then you walk through the forest for a while and start to descend to the coast. At about this point you will have the best view of several rock formations/islands in the sea, which are a symbol of the northern part of the Anaga coastline and can be seen from Punta del Hidalgo. The moment you see the Faro de Anaga lighthouse down ahead, get your knees properly greased. It's an endless descent down a dusty zig-zag path. The lighthouse itself is closed. But what is usually not closed, at least in high season, is a small pub on the coast near Roque Bermejo. I'd definitely recommend stocking up on water or other liquids here. It's a small detour from the ring road, but there are two black sand beaches in the village. Swimming in the sea on a hot African day definitely brought back our energy. The last and most time-consuming section is marked by exertion, sweat and endless hills. Still, I think it's a beautiful section. Climbing up the Barranco de Roque Bermejo canyon, with the ocean at our backs and not a soul in sight.
Punta Teno
Punta Teno is the westernmost tip of Tenerife and a small peninsula a few hundred metres long and a few tens of metres wide, jutting out into the Atlantic Ocean. On the Punta Teno peninsula stands a romantic red and white lighthouse, which is a very popular subject for sunset photos . Although the lighthouse on Punta Tenos can be reached directly by road from the town of Buenavista (about 10 km), the road is closed to private cars during the day between 9:00 and 20:00 Monday to Thursday, and Friday to Sunday the road is closed completely to private cars. However, bus line 369 runs several times a day from Buenavista to Punta Teno. You can also enjoy this trip on foot. The legendary scene from Fast and Furious 6 was filmed here.
Město La Orotava
One of the most charming towns in the Canary Islands, it boasts stunning steep streets, cobbled 'cat's heads', palaces, churches, but its main landmark is the wooden balconies of the town houses. A unique feature that is definitely worth a trip to La Orotava for is the Casa de los Balkones, the so-called House with Balconies. At first glance, you will be captivated by the bright façade adorned with heavy wooden doors, elegant windows and long balconies made of teak wood.
La Gomera
The island is mostly mountainous, with the highest mountains and Mount Garajonay (1,484 m) located in the central part of the island. The Garajonay National Park is also located here, where the original laurel forest grows. La Gomera is above all a paradise for tourists. You can start your stay by climbing the highest peak, Alto de Garajonay (1,484 m), which lies right in the middle. When the weather is good, you can enjoy spectacular views not only across La Gomera, but also far out to the ocean (and to another of the Canary Islands, La Palma and El Hierro). At night, you'll see amateur stargazers with telescopes at the viewpoint, as this island is free of light smog and the conditions for viewing celestial objects are almost ideal. La Gomera is characterised by deep valleys, rocky peaks, a misty haze over the wooded centre of the island and terraced fields for growing crops. The impressive nature and the lack of sandy beaches make the island particularly ideal for hiking. Local experts recommend the circular hiking route La Ruta 18 from the town of El Cedro (800 m above sea level), which is 17 kilometres long and has a total elevation gain of just under 1 000 metres. It is a pleasant six to eight hour trek (depending on how much you enjoy the surrounding countryside and how often you stop) with many views and, most importantly, a visit to the unique misty laurel forest in the national park. Interesting facts about the island The inhabitants of the island used to communicate by whistling, which is still partly used today, a good whistler can communicate from a distance of 3-4 km in good conditions. The area around Mirador de Igualero is also famous for the Silbo Gomero language, a special way of communicating by whistling that has been used for thousands of years on the island and is still taught in Gomer schools. On 6 September 1492, Christopher Columbus stopped on the island on his voyage across the Atlantic Ocean to discover the Americas, which is why La Gomera is also known as 'Columbus Island', because the famous Genoese sailor stopped here on his first voyage to the New World to replenish his supplies of fruit, vegetables and, above all, water. Many of the streets, houses and shops in the island's capital , San Sebastian, still bear Columbus' name. In the Plaza de la Constitución you can visit the original customs house, Casa de la Aguada, from whose wells the sailors drew drinking water before continuing their voyage.‍

Loro Park – nejlepší moderní zoologická zahrada na světě
The Loro Parque Zoo is located in Puerto de la Cruz in the north of Tenerife and is a unique place where you can see more than 400 species of animals up close and learn more about their characteristics and behaviour. In addition, the zoo became the first zoo in the world with a negative carbon footprint. Here you can observe monkeys, big cats or birds in their natural habitat, be surprised by the intelligence and skills of dolphins, killer whales and sea lions, walk among fish, sharks and all kinds of plants, and enjoy fun activities, restaurants and children's play areas for the little ones. Don't regret your money and go as early in the morning as possible. Loro Park is first and foremost a world unique collection of parrots, perhaps the largest in the world. That's how Loro Park came into being - as a collection of parrots. It wasn't until later that people started coming, so they added animals. Admission for a full day at Loro Parque costs €35 per person, €24 for a child. That's a pretty high price. On the other hand, I think it's worth it. Discounts are not given (not much reason, the park is crowded), except if you want to visit the park a second time - a second visit by the same person costs only 13Euro (you have to buy the ticket before you leave).
Siam Park
If you prefer unique entertainment to lounging by the water, then don't miss a visit to the popular Siam Park, a water theme park that has held the status of the largest, most modern and best water park of its kind in Europe for several years. SiamPark is much more than just a water park. Here you can enjoy rides on countless water slides, water slides and other water attractions that will keep you entertained all day long. Slide down the 28-metre high Tower of Power into the tunnel and ride through the huge aquarium full of sharks and rays, or surf in the pool with artificial waves up to 3 metres high, all in a beautiful and tropical park setting.
It is open from 1 May to 31 October from 10:00 to 18:00, and from 10:00 to 17:00 the rest of the year.
For adults over 11 years old: 38 EUR
Children 3-11 years: 26 EUR
Website: www.siampark.net
Siam Park is located in the Costa Adeje resort in the south of the island just off the motorway. It is impossible to miss it if you are driving, signs for Siam Park appear many kilometres before Adeje. Parking at the entrances is free. Bus number 424, which connects the resorts of Costa Adeje and Los Cristianos, also stops at the main entrance.

Secret places

SANATORIUM U ABADES
"Ghost town in Tenerife"
‍
Leprosy, or leprosy, was the most common disease on the island in the past. The disease was first encountered in the north, which was the most populated, and later a sanatorium was built on the south-east coast of Tenerife, just off Abades.
It was never completed, as at the same time advances in medicine arrived and the number of sufferers began to decrease. Later the area was used by the army for a time, but until now the whole place is deserted, painted with graffiti and the buildings are slowly becoming ruins.
‍
The nearest road to the mysterious sanatorium is to the left before entering the village of Abades, past the ravine separating the two places. The road is arid, rocky here and there, and will first lead you to a massive church with a cross above the main entrance. You can continue past other buildings that were meant to serve as a hospital, crematorium or administrative centre, and the rest is made up of many bungalows built side by side.
‍
The abandoned sanatorium at Abades can also be reached from the other side if you like longer walks and exploring new places.‍‍
CASA DE LA GORDEJUELA
"První parní stroj na Tenerife"
‍
‍La Gordejuela, také nazývaná Casa Hamilton byla významná průmyslová budova, postavená v těsné blízkosti pobřeží pod skalami. Nachází se na severu ostrova v oblasti Los Realejos a nedaleko města Puerto de La Cruz. K této zřícenině se dá dostat z nedaleké vesničky El Tostal, nebo dojít z vyhlídky San Pedro skrz chráněnou přírodní oblast Rambla de Castro, což je opravdu krásná procházka plná výhledů a palem.

‍This building was used as a water elevator, pumping water from below, then taking the elevator up to irrigate the banana plantations in the Orotava Valley. This building housed, among other things, the first steam engine in Tenerife. Now a ruin with no doors, windows or roof, it is reached by a long, winding stone staircase, but separated at the top by a gate with warning signs and no-entry signs. Despite this, some people find their way there, but due to the condition of the building it is not entirely safe. Still, just the view of this ruin alone is stunning.
LAS VENTANAS DE GÜIMAR
"Pěší túra vedoucí skrz tunely s "okny" v neuvěřitelné výšce."
‍
Jak se tam dostat?
‍Sjeďte z dálnice na Güimar a pokračujte do vnitrozemí. Musí se vyjet na skálu, která se tyčí nalevo od Güimaru a když narazíte na Mirador El Luchador, kde stojí masivní socha "bojovníka" musíte prudce odbočit doprava ještě před zatáčkou na úzkou silnici vedoucí uprostřed zatáčky naproti vyhlídce. Tato vozovka vede strmě nahoru a postupně se změní v prašnou cestu. Po chvíli uvidíte plošinu sloužící pro paragliding, kolem které pokračujte a zaparkovat doporučuji podél cesty co nejvýše to bude možné. Dávejte pozor, protože cesta je čím dál tím horší a pak to zvládnou jen terénní vozidla.
‍
Túra vede nejprve prudce nahoru, je náročnější a delší, než jsem čekala. Když se nahoře konečně dostanete do lesa, hledejte cestu napravo se žlutou výstražnou cedulí. Za ní již začíná cesta, která je ovšem na vlastní nebezpečí a rozhodně se nejedná o oficiální pěší zónu! Pokud se i tak odhodláte začít, cesta skrz křoviny vás navede k pozemnímu betonovému kanálu, který vede až k Las Ventanas de Güimar (Ventanas = "okna").

Když jsem tam byla já, počasí bylo dost pochmurné a po chvíli cesty podél kanálu, který vede na okraji skal, jsme byli obklopeni hustou mlhou. Nebylo vidět do okolí a jediné co jsem věděla je, že se nacházíme ve výšce kolem 1000 m n. m. a špatný krok by mohl znamenat pád. Najednou se před námi vynoří žlutý bagr připevněný ke skále a vedle něj první tmavý tunel, kterým jsme se museli vydat dál s rozsvícenou baterkou. Nedaleko již vcházíme do dalšího tunelu, kde konečně narážíme na trochu světla a ta očekávaná "Okna", což jsou vlastně díry ve skalním tunelu. Nabízejí krásné výhledy do okolí na další okna ve skalách a dolů do údolí Güimar spolu s výhledem na širý oceán.
‍
Las Ventanas de Güimar is one of the most unique places in Tenerife and I have to say I have never experienced a crazier hike full of adventure and adrenaline in my life!
EL SEMÁFORO
"Strážní věž na SV konci Tenerife."
‍
EL Semáforo se nachází na severovýchodním cípu Tenerife nad vesničkou Igueste. Je umístěn v nadmořské výšce vyšší než 200 m n. m. a patří k hornaté zalesněné oblasti zvané Anaga. Abyste se k němu dostali, musíte nejprve zdolat klikatou kamenitou cestu, která vede prudce nahoru z Igueste na skálu, kde se budova nachází. Cesta začíná za kostelem Igueste a správným směrem Vás navede šipka se značením na rozcestníku, který se nachází na úpatí hory.

Tato stavba dříve sloužila jako strážní věž k pozorování zejména lodní dopravy, k monitorování povětrnostních podmínek a předcházení všemožným incidentům, které jsou pozorovatelné zhora. Její výhodná pozice umožňovala zpozorovat z velké vzdálenosti lodě přijíždějící z Evropy a z věže je vidět i na kilometry daleko podél pobřeží až k hlavnímu městu Santa Cruz.

On the other side of the entrance gate is a hole in the fence, beyond which you'll find a path along the cliff with breathtaking views of Tenerife's uninhabited coastline.
LOS ROQUES DE FASNIA
"Opuštěná část vesničky s domečky postavenými přímo ve skále."
‍
Los Roques se nachází na východním pobřeží ostrova u sjezdu z dálnice do Fasnia. Hned na první pohled Vás upoutá benzínka postavená v neobvyklém stylu. Los Roques můžete najít hned za ní cestou dolů k oceánu.

U vesničky Los Roques se nachází pěkná pláž s černým pískem. Záhadné místo najdete napravo od této pláže, kde již spatříte skálu u pobřeží vystupující z oceánu. Přesně před ní stojí modrá obytná budova se zničeným bazénem a za ním tmavý tunel. Když ním projdete, najdete opuštěné náměstíčko s kaplí a domečky zasazenými přímo do skály. Zeď oddělující druhou část vesničky se dá přelézt ze strany a tím si můžete projít zbylou část, která je již víceméně obydlená.

I read in the news that Los Roques is still waiting to be rebuilt after one of the natural disasters that happened here in history. It could be a beautiful place with a boardwalk along two beaches separated by a cliff where one could walk through the village without a barricade. Well, until then, we can at least enjoy this deserted little square by the shore and enjoy the mysterious atmosphere that this place brings.

Events

Carneval Los Gigantes
From 22 February to 3 March, the Los Gigantes Carnival in Santiago del Teide returns with its legacy of fun. The main events are the Adult Queen's Choice Grand Gala and the vibrant Grand Parade of Carnival musicals. An exhibition of costumes from participating fantasies illustrates local creativity. The Drag Queen Grand Gala for Diversity and Artistic Equality promises an inclusive celebration. The sardine funeral, a classic event, marks the end of the festivities. This carnival, the oldest in the south of Tenerife, offers a varied programme for all ages. Don't miss it!
International carneval in Los Cristianos
Los Cristianos International Arona Carnival 2024 will take place from 7 to 18 March at the unique and special venues of Playa de Los Cristianos and Playa de Las Americas.

This 1980s-themed event is now a popular island, attracting tourists and establishing Arona as a tourist destination. Parades, galas and other events full of rhythm, colour and joy promise an unforgettable experience for all participants and spectators.
Carneval in Puerto de la Cruz
In the heart of July, Santa Cruz de Tenerife becomes the epicentre of musical magic and exquisite taste with the Cook Music Fest. This festival is a pure adrenaline rush for the senses, where culture, entertainment and the tastiest delicacies come together in a whirlwind of fun.

Imagine enjoying the chords of renowned artists, the laughter evoked by comedy shows and the excitement of performances designed especially for children. But that's not all! The gastronomic area offers fresh local ingredients and tasty dishes that are as beautiful as they are delicious. Warning: side effects may include uncontrollable finger tapping, contagious laughter and an insatiable appetite for deliciousness. Cook Music Fest isn't just a festival; it's a sensory smorgasbord that will have you singing, smiling and enjoying every moment.
